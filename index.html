About Me

Hi, I'm Travis! I graduated from Michigan State University with a bachelor's degree in computational mathematics, which is a little bit of computer science or more higher level math theory. I've been interested in technology since a young age, whether it be hardware or software. My interest in software development started when I took programming courses in high school and college. The assignments and projects seemed like putting puzzle pieces together, which is something I enjoy.

During college, I worked as a student assistant for Michigan State University IT, where I organized tickets for the network engineers and helped them with anything else they needed. An achievement of mine while working there was when I created a script using Python, Selenium, and openpyxl to 2mate a monthly and tedious task. The script cross-referenced tickets from our billing system and an Excel sheet, and improved our manual task time by 50%.

After graduating, I worked in a contract position as a software quality operations associate for Waymo. If you're not familiar with Waymo, it used to be Google's self-driving car project before the rebrand. In this role, I analyzed and reviewed visual data from self-driving cars and adjusted simulation metrics according to guidelines. I also learned how to use their internal tools, Jira, Google Workspace, and how to work in an agile environment.

I have perseverance towards my goals. My current goal is to become a software developer, and I've taken alternative educational routes towards this goal. I've been learning more about software development on my own through free online resources such as App Academy Open and C# Academy. Recently, I completed Cognizant's Java Full Stack Developer Certificate, where I learned more about Java, OOP, Spring, Maven, and more.

In my free time, I like to play video games, hang out with friends, go to the gym, and watch movies and TV shows.
